和食 - Washoku

The beauty of Washoku, or Japanese cuisine lies in its simplicity, elegance, and deep respect for ingredients.

Ingredients

The diet is rich in steamed rice, noodles, fish, tofu, seaweed, fresh, cooked, or pickled fruits and vegetables but low in added sugars and fats.

Seasons

Japan has four distinct seasons—spring, summer, autumn and winter—each with its own unique offerings of fruits, vegetables, seafoods and more.

Flavours

Japanese meals should contain a balance of salty, bitter, sweet, sour and savory or "umami". Umami is that elusive quality that is usually obtained by adding fish stock.

Dishes

寿司 - Sushi

consists of rice cooked with vinegar and fish or seafood, often raw.

茶碗蒸し - Chawanmushi

is a classic Japanese savory custard that is steamed in a delicate cup.

うどん - Udon

are chewy noodles made from wheat flour, typically served in a simple broth.

焼き鳥 - Yakitori

consists of bite-sized pieces of grilled chicken served on a bamboo skewer.

蕎麦- Soba

are thin noodles made from buckwheat, served chilled with a dipping sauce.

おにぎり - Onigiri

are rice balls made of steamed rice and are usually wrapped in a seaweed sheet.
